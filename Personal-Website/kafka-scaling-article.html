Scaling Kafka for Global Risk Engines

By Somasundar M • Sep 15, 2025 • 10 min read

Processing millions of transactions per second across global markets requires more than just a message broker—it requires a robust Kafka-driven ecosystem. Here are lessons learned from engineering scale at Citi.

1. Partitioning Strategy

Correct partitioning is the backbone of Kafka scalability. In our risk analytics platforms, we used composite keys (Branch + ProductID) to ensure even data distribution across brokers while maintaining order for critical calculations.

2. Monitoring with Prometheus & Grafana

You can't manage what you can't measure. We built custom dashboards to track consumer lag, broker throughput, and disk I/O metrics in OpenShift. This proactive approach reduced system downtime by 40%.

3. Schema Registry

Using Avro and the Confluent Schema Registry ensured that evolving data structures didn't break our downstream consumers, a critical requirement for multi-team collaborative engineering.

Scaling data systems is about resilience, monitoring, and clear architectural boundaries. These principles are what drive the high-fidelity performance of platforms like OmniTracker.
